what is halftone?

a process which uses dots of varying size and color
to produce one image. It includes the separation of
colors as seen above, and then the layering of the
colors creates an “optical illusion” which reflects
the original piece

halftone history

Created in the 1830’s by William Fox Talbot
as a solution to mixing colors.

the human printer

the halftone process was usually done by hand
(the human printer). Now it if done by hand,
it is considered a great work of art

pointillism

Developed by George Seurat in 1886, this is a painting
technique branching from impressionism in which
small, distinct dots of color are applied in patterns
to form an image. (Very similar to halftone)
